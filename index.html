Generative Quantum-inspired Kolmogorov-Arnold Eigensolver

Paper Implementation / Reproduction

A from-scratch reproduction of the GQKAE method, evaluated on the paper's H4 (4e, 4o) / 6-31G benchmark across a full potential-energy surface using NVIDIA CUDA-Q sampling and QSCI subspace diagonalization.

Original paper (PDF)

Abstract

The Generative Quantum-inspired Kolmogorov-Arnold Eigensolver (GQKAE) recasts ground-state estimation as a sequence-generation problem: a policy network proposes discrete operator sequences whose induced quantum circuits are sampled and post-processed by quantum-selected configuration interaction (QSCI). This page documents an independent reimplementation and reports its behaviour on the H4 linear-chain active-space benchmark.

Across a ten-point dissociation grid with five independent seeds per geometry (50 runs in total), the reimplementation reproduces the CASCI potential-energy surface to numerical precision and yields gate budgets consistent with the scale reported in the paper's H4 Table I. Introduction

Variational quantum eigensolvers optimise the continuous parameters of a fixed circuit ansatz. GQKAE instead treats the structure of the circuit as the object to be learned: a generative policy emits a sequence of discrete operator tokens, each sequence defines a circuit, and the quality of the resulting state is scored by the energy obtained after subspace diagonalization. Reinforcement learning then improves the policy. No continuous circuit angles are optimised in the loop.

This reimplementation follows the paper's design choices for the H4 active-space study: a UCCSD-derived Pauli-evolution operator pool, an HQKANsformer policy with quantum-inspired Kolmogorov-Arnold layers, CUDA-Q circuit sampling, QSCI with symmetry completion, and a Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) objective. The goal is reproduction and verification rather than novelty; the sections below report the method and the measured results.

Method

The training loop has four stages, repeated for a fixed number of iterations.

HQKANsformer and DARUAN activations

The policy is a compact transformer whose feed-forward blocks are quantum-inspired Kolmogorov-Arnold (QKAN) layers. Each edge carries a learnable univariate response built from re-uploaded sinusoidal features, the DARUAN edge bank, a classical, differentiable analogue of single-qubit data re-uploading. As shown in Section 7, this makes the model substantially more parameter-efficient than a comparable GQE transformer of equivalent vocabulary and sequence length.

QSCI: quantum-selected configuration interaction

Rather than estimating an expectation value shot by shot, the sampled measurement bitstrings define a set of computational-basis determinants. The molecular Hamiltonian is projected onto this subspace and diagonalized exactly. A symmetry-completion step enlarges the subspace to respect particle-number and spin symmetry; in the H4 active space this is sufficient to recover the CASCI eigenvalue (Section 4).

GRPO objective

Each sampled sequence is scored by its negative QSCI energy. The policy is trained with a token-wise clipped Group Relative Policy Optimization objective: advantages are normalised within each sampled batch, so the policy is pushed toward the best operator orderings discovered so far without a learned value function.

On the magnitude of the errors. The errors reported below (order 10⁻¹⁵ Ha) indicate that QSCI recovers the exact CASCI eigenvalue within the H4 (4e, 4o) active space. This is a faithful but deliberately small benchmark that mirrors the paper's setup; it demonstrates the full pipeline end-to-end and is not a claim of exceeding full configuration interaction.
